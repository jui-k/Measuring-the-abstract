Measuring The Abstract

Many studies strive to measure happiness and growth, but how? They are immeasurable, subjective, and abstract. And when looking for a place to move, it can be a good idea to be able to know something that has so far been considered abstract. Happiness could be perceived by the state of a person’s well-being, life satisfaction, etc. The most common approach, utilized by nearly all the reports we have read, summarizes surveys of self-reported measures of happiness. Who knows your state of happiness better than yourself?

In 2017, Gallup conducted 160,498 phone interviews nationally and they declare that their data projects to 95% of the US adult population. According to Gallup’s 2017 State of American Well-Being report, happiness can be measured with 5 attributes:
• PURPOSE: enjoying your daily life and having the motivation to achieve goals
• SOCIAL: having supportive and loving relationships
• FINANCIAL: managing your budget to reduce stress
• COMMUNITY: feeling safe, being proud of, and liking where you live
• PHYSICAL: being healthy and energetic

We collected the data from people living across Pune city, by asking various questions and calculated what the average score comes out to be, and compared it to the average score in various categories in the city. We do understand that surveys asking people about life satisfaction and happiness do measure subjective well-being with reasonable accuracy.

We used the data from PMC to make sure we also provide measurable data about the surroundings of the chosen location, like schools, hospitals, prices of homes, etc. So people can make more of an informed decision when planning to shift to another location.

We created a Google form and circulated it to collect primary data from people living in Pune city. Meanwhile, we downloaded data from the PMC website. We used python to clean the data in the CSV files, taking out the information relevant to this project and saved it in text files. We then proceeded to read the text files using PHP and showing the outputs depending upon the pin-code/area chosen by the user. We did all the calculations required including, counter, percentile, etc in PHP itself. Created a website using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript and embedded PHP in it to get the final result.

We hope that this project can help people make a more informed decision when looking for a new home, As it entails information that can make a huge difference in the overall living condition of the individual/family.
